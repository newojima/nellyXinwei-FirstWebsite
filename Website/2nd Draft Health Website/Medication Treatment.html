Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

Medication Treatments

Take Medications

Taking in medications can help you prevent further harmful effects of COPD and other diseases. For those who has no COPD, take medications that will help you prevent medical conditions and COPD, and maintain being healthy. For those who has COPD, your doctors will decide which medicines you need to take depending on the case or how severe the damages of your disease or COPD.

Oxygen

Oxygen is the best treatment for the people with COPD or who are having low-oxygen levels. People with COPD causes them to have difficulty to breathe air and out, and we need oxygen in our body. Oxygen is needed for our cells and body for respiration and the systems and organs that are working in our body.

Non-invasive ventilation

Non-invasive ventilation makes you wear either a nasal cannula or a short tube inserted in your nose, or a face mask connected to a ventilation machine that provides you more oxygen and push it to your lungs to help you breathe better. Your muscles will be more relax as this treatment will provide you more oxygen supply and help you exhale carbon dioxide which makes you easier to breathe.

Lung volume reduction surgery (LVRS)

Lung volume reduction surgery or LVRS is a surgery that can help the remaining healthy parts of your lungs to work better. This is best recommended to people who have emphysema or their aveolis (air sacs) are damaged. In these air sacs, gas exchange process takes place there with the capillaries binding them which is where the blood flows and exchanges the gas they carry (oxygen and carbon dioxide).

With LVRS, it will then help the healthy parts if the lungs to work better so you can breathe oxygen better since with emphysema or COPD, you can lack oxygen that is needed by your body and cells and some of your air sacs, damaged by these conditions don't work well or air becomes trapped because of loosing their elasticity that makes air in and out for gas exchange.

Lung transplant

Lung transplant may be encouraged if you have severe damages on lungs or severe COPD. This is only suitable for only few people depending on their condition and disease. You must know that lung transplants are risky operation as there are only a few donors of healthy lungs that can be also suitable donors of lungs to use as a replacement in the transplant.

Getting vaccinated

People with COPD who gets infections such as colds, flu, sinus infections and sore throats should get vaccinated and treat it quickly with their doctors. They must every year get vaccinated with "flu jabs" to be protected against flu viruses and vaccines against pneumococcal infections that can cause pneumonia and other conditions.
