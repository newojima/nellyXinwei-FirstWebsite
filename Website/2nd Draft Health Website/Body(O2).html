Our Body and Health

Oxygen and Cell Respiration

First and foremost, the most essential needs we need to survive and live are these three things: Food, Water, and Air. These 3 things are needed in our daily lives by our bodies and cells to perform many chemical processes for energy and nutrients. Without them, our cells, organs, systems, and bodies will no longer work, and they will starve and die as they have no energy to work and nutrients they need to work and be healthy.

Air is one of them, and that air, specifically, oxygen for our cells to do cell respiration. Cell Respiration is a chemical process done by the cells in our bodies to use oxygen and other forms of stored energy such as glucose so energy or ATP for our cells will be produced.

What is ATP? Adenosine triphosphate or ATP is a small molecule or coenzyme used by cells to perform other cell processes such as cell division, cell work for the body, organs, and systems to continue to work.

Cell Respiration is a set of multiple reactions and processes that involves the breakdown of complex molecules and nutrients with oxygen to be converted to ATP or a form of biochemical energy that will help them do their cell work in the systems, organs that will make our bodies alive, healthy, and working.
