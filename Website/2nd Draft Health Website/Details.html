Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

What is COPD?

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease is interpreted as a group of lung conditions that makes airways narrowed thus, the lungs will have a difficult time to empty out air. In other words, people with COPD will have a hard time to breathe air, making the lungs unable to take in oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide.

This medical condition damages and lessens the pull of the lung tissues in the airways as their elastic lining flops and get inflamed, making the passage of the air narrowed or less air will go in which makes it harder to breathe and have low-oxygen levels.
