Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

Lifestyle Treatments

Don't smoke

Since inhaling smoke or smoke cigarettes are likely the main cause of COPD, both smokers or non-smokers should not smoke. Smoke can give many harmful effects on the lungs and you can get various diseases from that. COPD is one of the many diseases you can get from it. If you have COPD and you still smoke, the best treatment is to stop smoking to prevent any further pulmonary damages that will affect your lungs and health. For those who don't smoke, prevent smoking to avoid having COPD, other diseases, and harmful effects that affects ones health from it.

Have a plan

Managing one's lifestyle or having a plan can help you both prevent or if you have this disease, manage living with COPD and being breathless. Since the damages of this disease can't be reversed and it is chronic, having a plan can slow down the damages this disease brings. For people who has COPD, you should have a regular checkups with your doctors or a health care professionals. For those who has no COPD yet, having a healthy plan and getting regular checkups can help you prevent this and further conditions.

Healthy Diet

Having a healthy diet and weight can be helpful to manage and prevent further damages and diseases that affects your health. Have a hearty, healthy diet with the essential nutrients you need is important for one. If you have COPD, ask your doctor to help you give suggestions to have a healthy plan of diet and find the nutrients you need to manage your weight and disease. If you are overweight, it will be harder for you to breathe and move around. You should not skip meals or eat less, you should eat enough more often to gain enough nutrients to be healthy.

Exercise

People with COPD should exercise because exercising is like a rehabilitation for the lungs, from the damages brought by this disease. Exercising can help you improve your breathing and quality of life and health. Regular exercises can strengthen the muscles, benefits the systems in our body, and be healthy enough to be immune to other diseases that can threaten our health.

If you have COPD, you should take part in the pulmonary rehabilitation, it provides education and exercises, some suggestions and advices that can help improve or manage living with COPD.
