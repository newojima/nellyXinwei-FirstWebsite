Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

Causes and Risk Factors

Smoking and Inhaling Pollutants

About 90% of individuals who has COPD are 40 years and above who had history of smoking or smoked before. Smoking, Secondhand smoking, and inhaling pollutants are likely the main causes of COPD. Smoke can irritate our lungs and can bring other diseases of the respiratory system aside from COPD, and this can be more dangerous as it brings severe pulmonary damages.

Environmental Factors

People living in long period of time with contact in an environment and workplaces with harmful pollutants are also in risk of having COPD. Fumes, chemicals, and the dust in the air, polluting the environment they live at are the harmful pollutants that causes them to have this disease because this also irritates and damage our lungs.

Genetic Factors

Lacking of Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Protein (AATD) in bloodstream causes COPD. Without these proteins, white blood cells harm the lungs and lung deterioration occurs. Other than having AATD, there are other predispositions and genetical factors that develops or can be a cause of COPD to other people.
