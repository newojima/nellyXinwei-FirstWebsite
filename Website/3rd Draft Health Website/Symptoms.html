Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

Symptoms of COPD

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary diseases affects one, causing them to have symptoms of having difficulty in breathing and more as this chronic disease persist to damage the lungs and the respiratory system. These symptoms are:
  • Being breathless or lacking oxygen and energy in the body

  • Frequent coughing and producing more phlegm or sputum than usual.

  • Wheezing and feeling of uncomfortable tightness in chest.

  • Frequent respiratory infections such as colds and flu.

  • Heart beats faster and bluish or gray fingernails (mean lacking oxygen circulation).

Diagnosis and Tests for Checking-up for COPD

If you feel any of these kinds of symptoms, ask your doctor for check up and you will be taking a "spirometry test" (used for measuring breath), some lung function, x-ray or visualising tests, and even blood tests.
